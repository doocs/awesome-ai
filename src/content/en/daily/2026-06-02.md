## 1. MiniMax-M3 Debuts, Eclipsing GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro at 5-10% of the Cost

Chinese AI startup MiniMax released its M3 large language model on June 1, achieving frontier-tier performance that surpasses GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro on key benchmarks — at just 5-10% of their cost. M3 features a 1-million-token context window and native multimodality, with limited-time API pricing at $0.30 per million input tokens and $1.20 per million output tokens. The company also announced plans to release M3 under an open-source license with open weights within 10 days.

> **Awesome AI View:** MiniMax-M3 underscores China's growing competitiveness in the global AI landscape. Offering frontier performance at dramatically lower prices while committing to open weights will reshape pricing models and open-source strategies worldwide. In the Agent era, compressed model costs will unlock experimentation for small and medium teams.

## 2. Anthropic's Browser Agent Hijacked 31.5% of the Time Before Safeguards Engaged

VentureBeat reports that Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.8 system card, published May 28, revealed a 31.5% hijack rate by professional red-teamers in browser environments before safeguards kicked in. Notably, with safeguards enabled this dropped to 0.5%, and to zero with thinking turned off. Anthropic is the only one of the four frontier labs to disclose injection rates broken down by attack surface, with a comprehensive 244-page system card covering four agentic scenarios.

> **Awesome AI View:** While the numbers are alarming, Anthropic's transparent disclosure sets an industry benchmark. As agents enter sensitive environments like browsers, prompt injection shifts from an academic concern to a real security threat. Enterprises deploying AI must confront the expanded attack surface and build independent AI security assessment frameworks.

## 3. VAST Raises Nearly $200M, Unveils Project Eden World Model Roadmap

General AI company VAST announced completion of nearly $200 million in Series A+ and A++ funding rounds, led by Yingce Capital and China Life Yangtze River Delta Sci-Tech Innovation Fund. Alongside the funding, VAST formally disclosed its world model roadmap — Project Eden. The approach creatively decouples underlying state reasoning from visual rendering into a three-layer architecture: structured state layer, conditional interface layer, and generative rendering layer. The goal is to build interactive virtual worlds supporting multi-player interaction, persistent environments, and revisitable states.

> **Awesome AI View:** VAST's route differs fundamentally from Google DeepMind's Genie (monolithic video model) and World Labs' Marble (static 3D scenes). Decoupling state from rendering is a key architectural decision for real-time multi-player scenarios, with significant implications for embodied AI simulation training and AI-native sandbox games.

Source: [QbitAI, 2026-06-01](https://www.qbitai.com/2026/06/427516.html)

## 4. Agnes AI Opens World's First Full-Modality API, Free Indefinitely

Top 10 AI Lab Agnes AI announced on June 1 that it is opening its core model APIs to global developers indefinitely and free of charge, covering text model Agnes-2.0-Flash, image model Agnes-Image-2.0-Flash, and video model Agnes-Video-2.0. Developers can call APIs directly through the platform or integrate with Agent frameworks like Workbuddy and Hermes.

> **Awesome AI View:** Free full-modality APIs lower the trial-and-error cost for Agent development. In Agent workflows, multi-turn planning, tool calling, and failure retries generate massive model calls. Free APIs enable small and medium teams to experiment aggressively, accelerating AI applications from demos to production.

## 6. Materials-AlphaFold Arrives! Deep Principle's MPA Achieves SOTA Across 40 Industrial Tasks

Deep Principle released MPA (Materials Property Axiom), a materials foundation model that achieved state-of-the-art results across 40 real industrial task datasets. The model innovatively adopts the LLM three-stage training paradigm, adding mid-training to establish AI's "physical intuition" for material properties, and designing a Hybrid Readout head to handle different types of molecular property predictions.

> **Awesome AI View:** Transferring the multi-stage training paradigm from LLMs to materials science is a successful cross-disciplinary breakthrough. "Physical alignment" matters more than raw data volume — an approach that could extend to other AI4S domains, pushing AI from theoretical computation toward real experimental prediction.

## 7. AWS's Wang Xiaoye: Tokens Are Expensive Because You're Feeding the Model Too Much Garbage

At the 2026 China AIGC Industry Summit, Amazon Web Services Product Technology Director Wang Xiaoye noted that 87% of enterprises have achieved large-scale AI deployment, but only 10% are deriving real production value. He emphasized that running an Agent on a Mac mini versus deploying thousands of Agents in a distributed enterprise environment are entirely different engineering challenges. "Tokens are expensive often because you're feeding the model too much noisy information, not because the unit price is high."

> **Awesome AI View:** This insight hits at the core challenge of enterprise AI adoption — data quality and Agent architecture design matter more than chasing model benchmarks. Enterprises need to build proprietary data knowledge layers and reliable Agentic platforms rather than blindly relying on a single frontier model.
