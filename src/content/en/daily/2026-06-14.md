## 1. White House Imposes Export Controls on Anthropic, Bans Foreign Access to Fable and Mythos Models

On June 13, 2026, the White House ordered Anthropic to immediately suspend access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models for all foreign nationals. The action was triggered by a security research report submitted to the U.S. government by Amazon, raising concerns about advanced AI technology potentially being accessed through foreign channels. Anthropic has halted all new AI tool releases pending government security review.

Sources: [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/949601/amazon-anthropic-fablemythos-government-ban), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/13/anthropic-amazon-white-house)

> **Awesome AI View:** This marks the first time the U.S. has imposed export controls on large language models, signaling that AI technology is now formally treated as a national security asset. While the move addresses legitimate security concerns, it raises questions about the future of the open AI ecosystem—will technical controls accelerate the fragmentation of global AI development?
