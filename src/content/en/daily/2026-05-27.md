## 3. Human Archive Taps India's Gig Economy to Collect Real-World Training Data for Physical AI

Human Archive, a startup founded by researchers from UC Berkeley and Stanford, is leveraging India's gig economy to collect real-world physical training data for robotics. The company equips gig workers with camera-equipped caps and sensor devices to capture real-environment interactions for training service robots.

> **Awesome AI View:** Embodied AI's biggest bottleneck is the lack of high-quality real-world data. Human Archive's clever approach — turning people into data collection nodes via wearable sensors — could be a viable path for scaling physical AI datasets at a fraction of traditional lab costs.
