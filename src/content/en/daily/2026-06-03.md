## 1. Trump Signs Narrower AI Oversight Executive Order, Requiring Pre-Release Model Review

On June 2, President Trump signed a scaled-back executive order requiring AI models to undergo review before public release. The order was modified following strong objections from the tech industry, narrowing the scope of the original version. This executive order marks the formal institutionalization of federal AI safety oversight in the US, requiring major AI companies to submit safety assessments before deploying frontier models.

Source: [TechCrunch, 2026-06-02](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/trump-signs-narrower-executive-order-on-ai-oversight-after-industry-objections/) | [The Verge, 2026-06-02](https://www.theverge.com/policy/941775/trump-ai-executive-order)

> **Awesome AI View:** AI regulation is shifting from industry self-governance to government mandates. While this order is narrower than the initial draft, the establishment of "pre-release review" as a core requirement means safety assessments will become a compliance standard. Companies need to build more robust safety review processes, and regulators must develop sufficient technical evaluation capabilities.

## 4. Google Phone App Rolls Out AI Deepfake Call Detection

On June 2, Google announced a fake call detection feature in its Phone app, using AI to identify and block voice impersonation scams powered by deepfakes. When suspicious calls are detected, the system provides real-time warnings to users, especially when the caller appears to be impersonating a contact. This is Google's important security measure against AI voice cloning technology abuse.

Source: [TechCrunch, 2026-06-02](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/google-rolls-out-fake-call-detection-to-protect-against-ai-deepfake-impersonation-scams/) | [The Verge, 2026-06-02](https://www.theverge.com/tech/941517/google-phone-scammer-ai-impersonation)

> **Awesome AI View:** AI-driven defense against AI-driven attacks — this is the inevitable trajectory of AI security. Google's system-level advantage allows it to integrate deepfake detection directly into the Phone app, a significant practice in platform-level security. As AI voice cloning becomes widespread, this "AI vs. AI" protection will become a basic OS feature.

## 6. Hackers Exploit Meta AI Support Chatbot to Steal Celebrity Instagram Accounts

On June 1, Ars Technica reported that hackers successfully deceived Meta's AI support chatbot, exploiting a vulnerability to gain access to celebrity Instagram accounts. Hijacked accounts were subsequently sold. Meta has since patched the vulnerability, but the incident exposes serious security flaws in AI customer service systems regarding identity verification and access control.

Source: [Ars Technica, 2026-06-01](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/06/meta-ai-support-chatbot-gave-hackers-access-to-notable-instagram-accounts/) | [The Verge, 2026-06-01](https://www.theverge.com/tech/941179/meta-instagram-ai-support-chatbot-exploit-hacked)

> **Awesome AI View:** This incident is a classic AI security cautionary tale — when AI systems are granted high-privilege operational capabilities without strict identity verification and permission boundaries, they become tools for attackers. AI Agent security design must follow the "principle of least privilege." Customer service AI should never have account operation permissions that bypass human review.
