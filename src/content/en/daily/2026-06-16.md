## 1. Salesforce Acquires AI Customer Service Platform Fin for $3.6B

Salesforce announced its acquisition of AI customer service platform Fin for $3.6 billion, integrating Fin's team and technology into its Agentforce platform. Agentforce is Salesforce's enterprise platform that businesses use to build custom AI agents for automating tasks. Fin is known for its advanced AI conversation engine and automated customer support solutions, making this a strategic move to strengthen Salesforce's position in enterprise AI customer service.

Source: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/15/salesforce-acquires-ai-customer-service-platform-fin-for-3-6b/) | 2026-06-15

> **Awesome AI View:** The $3.6B price tag reflects the valuation premium in the AI customer service sector. As LLM infrastructure becomes increasingly commoditized, vertical AI applications — especially those with mature customer bases and clear ROI — are becoming the most direct acquisition targets for tech giants. Salesforce is continuing its "buy capability" rather than "build capability" strategy.

This represents Meta's latest effort to catch up in the AI race and keep users more engaged on the platform.

Source: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/15/metas-new-ai-mode-on-facebook-pulls-from-public-info-across-its-platforms/) | 2026-06-15

> **Awesome AI View:** Meta's unique advantage lies in its massive user data and cross-platform ecosystem. "AI Mode" essentially transforms the social graph into a data moat for AI search. But the privacy boundary question remains — the tension between public information and user expectations will be a focal point for regulators.

The experts noted that cutting-edge AI models are critical tools for discovering and fixing vulnerabilities, and restricting access puts "the good guys" at a disadvantage.

Source: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/15/cybersecurity-vets-protest-dangerous-us-government-ban-on-anthropics-most-powerful-models/) | 2026-06-15

This debate will directly shape future AI security policy — should the approach be "restrict the tool" or "arm the defenders"?

## 9. Sakana AI Launches Marlin: Autonomous Research Agent Based on AB-MCTS

Tokyo-based Sakana AI shipped its first commercial product, Sakana Marlin, an autonomous research agent powered by AB-MCTS (Adversarial Monte Carlo Tree Search). Marlin can generate research reports of up to 100 pages along with presentation slides, marking a significant step in the transition of autonomous AI research tools from proof-of-concept to commercialization.

Source: [Marktechpost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/15/sakana-ai-marlin/) | 2026-06-15

> **Awesome AI View:** Sakana AI's application of research-domain search algorithms (MCTS variants) to enterprise report generation demonstrates the potential of cross-domain algorithm transfer. When AI agents can independently complete the full research-analysis-presentation workflow, the paradigm of knowledge work undergoes a fundamental shift.

## 10. OpenAI Says "Entirely Automating Everything Is Not the Future We Want"

OpenAI has walked back its goal of fully autonomous AI research by 2028, now emphasizing a "tandem" approach between humans and machines. Sam Altman and Jakub Pachocki have called for the creation of an international body that could slow frontier AI development if necessary. This marks a shift from OpenAI's previously aggressive automation stance toward a more cautious position.

Source: [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/openai-says-entirely-automating-everything-is-not-the-future-we-want/) | 2026-06-15

> **Awesome AI View:** OpenAI's shift in stance is significant — as AI capabilities accelerate toward critical thresholds, calls for "braking" have moved from academia to the industry core. While the proposal for an international AI governance body is idealistic, without global coordination mechanisms, the "prisoner's dilemma" of AI competition will persist.
